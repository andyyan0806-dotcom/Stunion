### Parent Interview Template (Updated for No-Video Trust Test)

```
Date:
Name (anonymized):
School:
Child's grade:
Current tutoring spend (₩/month):

PART A - Current behavior
1. How do you currently find SAT/IB tutors?
2. What did you pay for your last tutor (₩/hour)?
3. What went wrong with past tutors?
4. How did you verify the tutor was qualified before booking?

PART B - Trust threshold (CRITICAL, no leading)
5. Imagine a platform shows you a tutor's verified university transcript,
   verified test scores, and reviews from past students. Your payment is
   held in escrow until you confirm the session went well.
   - Would you book a tutor you have never met based on this alone?
   - If no, what additional information would you need?
   - Would a 15-minute video call with the tutor before booking change
     your answer?

PART C - Willingness to pay
6. Tutor charges ₩100,000/hour. Platform takes 10% (₩10,000) as a fee.
   Would you use the platform or contact the tutor directly?
7. What would make you stay on the platform after the first session?

PART D - Open
8. Anything else about tutoring in Korea that frustrates you?
```

### Trust Threshold Scoring

After 10 interviews, count answers to Question 5:

| Result | Implication |
|---|---|
| 7+ would book without meeting | Trust mechanism works. Proceed to build. |
| 4-6 would book, rest need intro call | Build with 15-min intro call feature as P0. |
| <4 would book without meeting | Trust mechanism insufficient. Reconsider video, or pivot to Path B. |

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid This Week

- Building anything (no code, no design, no logo) before validation completes
- Adjusting questions to fish for the answers you want
- Treating polite "yeah maybe I'd try it" responses as validation (require concrete pain + concrete willingness to pay)
- Telling people "I'm building a tutor marketplace" instead of "I want to learn how you find tutors today"
- **NEW:** Skipping Part B of the interview because it feels uncomfortable to ask. Trust threshold is now the highest-risk unknown. Do not skip.
